The MIT "Funny Algorithm" has Analyzed Over 80 Million Videos
About

How many social scientists does it take to explain what makes something funny? It turns out that the answer is many. Although a number of theories have been offered to explain the concept of humor, what humor really is has remained elusive… until now. The MIT “Funny Algorithm,” developed by social scientists at the Massachusetts Institute of Technology, scores comedic performance along a number of dimensions, including delivery, originality, style, pace, and content.

This algorithm was trained on 80 million publicly available video clips following the self-supervised learning method that is well-established in the field of artificial intelligence. This algorithm is the first of its kind aimed at quantifying humor.

Algorithm use should be for educational or entertainment purposes only. The results of this tool are not psychological or psychiatric advice of any kind. Due to the evolving nature of this algorithm, we note that results are not without margins of error of orders of magnitude. Data from tool use will be recorded anonymously (without any identifying information), and may be used for research.
